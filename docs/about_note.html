About Pazator

a note from the developer ; AIX

hiiii, it's AIX here, the person behind this whole thing. i made pazator as a fun project. not because i needed it, not because someone paid me to, but because i wanted to see if i could. and honestly? it turned into something way bigger than i expected.

the idea came from seeing palantir, you know, that big intelligence platform that costs millions and needs a whole team to run. i thought, "how hard could it be to make something similar?" turns out: really hard. but also not impossible. so here we are, a solo dev on a laptop, with something that actually works.

pazator isnt meant to replace palantir or compete with it. its more like a proof of concept that one person can build complex tools if they care enough to try. the graph view, the ontology designer, the pipeline system, the gis tab; none of it was planned from the start. it just grew as i kept adding things that felt useful.

you know i had to remove alot because it felt like a toy, it still does just less. thats the proplem with trying to building something so big.

i'm not a professional software engineer. i dont have a cs degree. i just learn stuff as i go. the codebase is messy, and some parts of the ui are held together with duct tape. but it works.

where did you get this idea?
i saw palantir and thought "that looks cool, i want one." so i made one. well, my version of one. palantir is obviously on another level ; thousands of engineers, decades of development, military-grade infrastructure. my version runs in a browser tab and stores data in indexeddb. but the core idea is the same: connect dots, find patterns, make sense of information.

is this actually useful?
Ehhh, not for anything important, it still aint doing much, i mean its a tool ment for goverments how the fuck am i supoused to get the data? oh ya thats a big bottleneck in the app, the data.

whats next?
i keep adding stuff when i have time. the collab module is half-finished, the reports system needs work, and i keep finding bugs in the css.

can i contribute?
probably not in the traditional sense since this is a personal project. but if you find bugs or have ideas, feel free to reach out.

Do you like coding?
No, like, i hate coding. but i like making things. so we be coding.

AIX, artificial intractive experience, thats the name, none is reading this.
